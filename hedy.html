Women in Computing

Hedy Lamarr

As a famous actress, not many think of Hedy Lamarr as a significant inventor. But during World War II, she developed a broad-spectrum frequency hopping technology with composer George Antheil. This technology was adopted by the US military in the 1960s and is the precursor to our modern day Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and CDMA technologies.
